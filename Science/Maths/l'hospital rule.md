Suppose $f$ and $g$ are [[derivatives|differentiable]] and $g'(x) \neq 0$ on an open interval $I$ that contains $a$ (except possibly at $a$). Suppose that $$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{x \to a} g(x) = 0$$ or that $$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \pm\infty \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{x \to a} g(x) = \pm\infty$$ (In other words, we have an [[indeterminate forms|indeterminate form]] of type $\frac{0}{0}$ or $\infty/\infty$.) Then $$\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \lim_{x \to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)}$$ if the [[limit]] on the right side exists (or is $\infty$ or $-\infty$).
