If $f$ is [[continuity|continuous]] on $[a, b]$, then the [[function]] $g$ defined by $$g(x) = \int_{a}^{x}f(t)dt \quad a \leqslant x \leqslant b$$
is [[continuity|continuous]] on $[a, b]$ and [[derivatives|differentiable]] on a $(a, b)$, and $g'(x) = f(x)$

---
If $f$ is [[continuity|continuous]] on $[a, b]$, then $$\int_{a}^{b}f(x)dx = F(b) - F(a)$$ where $F$ is any [[derivatives|antiderivative]] of $f$, that is, [[function]] such that $F' = f$
