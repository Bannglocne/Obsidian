Let $S$ be a solid that lies between $x = a$ and $x = b$. If the cross-sectional [[area]] of $S$ in the plane $P_x$, through $x$ and perpendicular to the $x$-axis, is $A(x)$, where $A$ is a [[continuity|continuous]] [[function]], then the volume of $S$ is $$V = \lim_{n \to \infty} \Sigma_{i = 1}^{n}A(x^{*}_{i}) \Delta x = \int_{a}^{b}A(x)dx$$

# solids of revolution
$$A = \pi(\text{outer radious})^2 - \pi(\text{innter radius})^2$$

# pyramid
$$
V = \int_0^h \frac{L^2}{h^2}(h - y)^2dy= \frac{L^2h}{3}
$$

# cylindrical shells
$$
V = 2 \pi r h \Delta r
$$
or
$$
V = \int_a^b2\pi xf(x)dx \text{ where } 0 \leqslant a < b
$$
and it can be remembered as
$$
V = [\text{circumference}][\text{height}][\text{thickness}]
$$
