#calculus #maths 
> The **natural logarithmic [[function]]** is the [[function]] defined by

$$\ln x = \int_1^x \frac{1}{t} \, dt \quad \quad x > 0$$

---
**Laws**
 If $x$ and $y$ are positive numbers and $r$ is a rational number, then 
  1. $\ln(xy) = \ln x + \ln y$ 
 2. $\ln\left(\frac{x}{y}\right) = \ln x - \ln y$ 
 3. $\ln(x^r) = r \ln x$
---
[[derivatives]] and [[integral]]
$$
\frac{d}{dx}(\ln x) = \frac{1}{x} \quad ;\quad \frac{d}{dx}(\ln u) = \frac{1}{x} \frac{du}{dx} \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{d}{dx}[\ln g(x)] = \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)}
$$
$$
\frac{d}{dx}(\ln|x|) = \frac{1}{x} \quad ; \quad \int \frac{1}{x}dx = \ln |x| + C
$$
---
[[limit]]
$$
\lim_{x \to \infty} \ln x = 0 \quad \quad \lim_{x \to 0+} \ln x = -\infty
$$
---
[[e]]
_e_ is the number such that $\ln e = 1$
