#calculus #maths 
The [[logarithmic functions|logarithm]] with base [[e]] is called the **natural logarithm**
> $\log_ex = \ln x$

$$
\ln x = y \Leftrightarrow e^y = x
$$

$$
\ln (e^x) = x \quad x \in \mathbb{R}
$$

$$
e^{\ln x} = x \quad x > 0
$$

$$
\ln e = 1
$$

---
**[[limit]]**
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \ln x = \infty \quad \quad \lim_{x \to 0^+} \ln x = -\infty$$
---
**[[derivatives]]**
$$
\frac{d}{dx} (\ln x) = \frac{1}{x} \quad \quad \frac{d}{dx}(\ln |x|) = \frac{1}{x}
$$
$$
\frac{d}{dx} (\ln u) = \frac{1}{u} \frac{du}{dx}
$$
$$
\frac{d}{dx}[\ln g(x)] = \frac{g'(x)}{g(x)}
$$
$$
\int \tan x dx = \ln |\sec x| + C
$$
