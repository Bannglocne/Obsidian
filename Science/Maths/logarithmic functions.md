#maths #principles #calculus 
> $log_ax = y \Leftrightarrow a^y = x$
[[inverse function|Inverse function]] of [[exponential function]]

$$
log_a(a^x) = x \text { for every x} \in \mathbb{R}
$$

$$
a^{log_ax} = x \text { for every x > 0}
$$

---
**Laws**
f $a > 1$, the [[function]] $f(x) = \log_a x$ is a one-to-one, continuous, increasing [[function]] with domain $(0, \infty)$ and range $\mathbb{R}$. If $x, y > 0$ and $r$ is any real number, then
1. $\log_a(xy) = \log_a x + \log_a y$
2. $\log_a\left(\frac{x}{y}\right) = \log_a x - \log_a y$
3. $\log_a(x^r) = r\log_a x$

---
**[[limit]]**
If $a > 1$, then
$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \log_a x = \infty \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{x \to 0^+} \log_a x = -\infty$$

---
**Change of Base Formula**
$$
log_ax = \frac{\ln x}{\ln a}
$$
---
**[[derivatives]]**
$$
\frac{d}{dx}(log_ax) = \frac{1}{x \ln a}
$$
