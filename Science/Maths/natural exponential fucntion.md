The [[exponential function]] with base [[e]] is called **natural exponential fucntion**

The [[exponential function]] $f(x) = e^x$ is an increasing [[continuity|continuous]] [[function]] with domain $\mathbb{R}$ and range $(0, \infty)$. Thus $e^x > 0$ for all $x$. Also

$$\lim_{x \to -\infty} e^x = 0 \quad \quad \lim_{x \to \infty} e^x = \infty$$

So the $x$-axis is a horizontal [[asymptotes|asymptote]] of $f(x) = e^x$.

---
**Laws:**
If $x$ and $y$ are real numbers and $r$ is rational, then
1. $e^{x+y} = e^x e^y$
2. $e^{x-y} = \frac{e^x}{e^y}$
3. $(e^x)^r = e^{rx}$


---
**[[derivatives]]**
$$
\frac{d}{dx}(e^x) = e^x \quad ; \quad \frac{d}{dx}(e^u) = e^u \frac{du}{dx}
$$
---
**[[integral]]**
$$
\int e^x dx = e^x + c
$$
