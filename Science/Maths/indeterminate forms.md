#calculus #maths 
A [[limit]] of the form is called an **indeterminate form**
1.  of type $\frac{0}{0}$
$$
\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}
$$
where both $f(x) \to 0$ and $g(x) \to 0$ as $x \to a$

---
2. of type $\infty / \infty$
$$
\lim_{x \to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)}
$$
where both $f(x) \to \infty$ and $g(x) \to \infty$ as $x \to a$
---

3. of type $0.\infty$
$$
\lim_{x \to a}[f(x)g(x)]
$$
where $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = 0$ and $\lim_{x \to a}g(x) = \infty (\text{or} -\infty)$. 

---
4. of type $\infty - \infty$
$$
\lim_{x \to a}[f(x) - g(x)]
$$
where $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \infty$ and $\lim_{x \to a}g(x) = \infty$. 

---
5. Indeterminate Power
$$\lim_{x \to a} [f(x)]^{g(x)}$$

- $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{x \to a} g(x) = 0 \qquad \text{type } 0^0$

- $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = \infty \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{x \to a} g(x) = 0 \qquad \text{type } \infty^0$

- $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = 1 \quad \text{and} \quad \lim_{x \to a} g(x) = \pm\infty \qquad \text{type } 1^\infty$
