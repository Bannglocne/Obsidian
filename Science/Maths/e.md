#principles #maths #calculus 
> e is the number such that $\lim_{h \to 0}\frac{e^h - 1}{h} = 1$

**[[derivatives]]**:
$$
\frac{d}{dx}(e^x) = e^x
$$
**with [[the chain rule|Chain rule]]**
$$
\frac{d}{dx}(e^u) = e^u\frac{du}{dx}
$$

**as a [[limit]]**
$$
e = \lim_{x \to 0}(1 + x)^{\frac{1}{x}} = \lim_{n \to \infty}(1 + \frac{1}{n})^n
$$
