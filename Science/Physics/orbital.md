#physics #quantum #chemistry #orbital 
> An **atomic orbital** is a three-dimensional region around an [[atoms|atomic]] nucleus where there is a high probability (typically 90-95%) of finding an electron. It represents a specific [[energy]] state and [[wave functions]], holding a maximum of two electrons with opposite _spins_.

To describe an orbital, we use [[quantum numbers]].

# Orbital Diagrams
> Each orbital is denoted by a box and each electron by a half arrow. A half arrow pointing up/down  represents an electron with a positive/negative [[electron spin|spin]] magnetic [[quantum numbers|quantum number]]. 

$$
\text{Li} \quad
\begin{array}{c}
\boxed{\textcolor{red}{\uparrow \downarrow}} \\
1s
\end{array}
\quad
\begin{array}{c}
\boxed{\textcolor{red}{\uparrow}\phantom{\downarrow}} \\
2s
\end{array}
$$

There trends:
1. The number of peaks increases with increasing n, with the outermost peak being larger than inner ones. 
2. The number of nodes increases with increasing n. 
3. The electron density becomes more spread out with increasing n.
