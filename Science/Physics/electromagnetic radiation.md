#wave #physics #electromagnetism
> **Electromagnetic radiation** consists of waves of the electromagnetic field, propagating through space, carrying electromagnetic radiant [[energy]].



This inverse relationship between the [[frequency]] and [[wavelength]] of electromagnetic radiation is expressed by the equation:
$$
c = \lambda v
$$
where $c$ is the speed of light, $\lambda$ is [[wavelength]], $v$ (nu) is [[frequency]]

| **Unit**   | **Symbol** | **Length (m)** | **Type of Radiation** |
| ---------- | ---------- | -------------- | --------------------- |
| Angstrom   | Å          | $10^{-10}$     | X-ray                 |
| Nanometer  | nm         | $10^{-9}$      | Ultraviolet, visible  |
| Micrometer | $\mu$m     | $10^{-6}$      | Infrared              |
| Millimeter | mm         | $10^{-3}$      | Microwave             |
| Centimeter | cm         | $10^{-2}$      | Microwave             |
| Meter      | m          | 1              | Television, radio     |
| Kilometer  | km         | 1000           | Radio                 |
