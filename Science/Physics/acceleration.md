#physics #kinematics 
**Average acceleration** is the rate at which [[velocity]] changes; is a [[vector]] in the same direction as the change in [[velocity]], $\Delta v$ $$\bar{a} = \frac{\Delta v}{\Delta t} = \frac{v_f - v_0}{t_f - t_0}$$

**Instantaneous Acceleration**:
$$\vec{\mathbf{a}}(t) = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{\vec{\mathbf{v}}(t + \Delta t) - \vec{\mathbf{v}}(t)}{\Delta t} = \frac{d\vec{\mathbf{v}}(t)}{dt}$$
The acceleration in terms of components is
$$\vec{\mathbf{a}}(t) = \frac{dv_x(t)}{dt}\hat{\mathbf{i}} + \frac{dv_y(t)}{dt}\hat{\mathbf{j}} + \frac{dv_z(t)}{dt}\hat{\mathbf{k}}$$
The acceleration in terms of the second derivative of the position [[function]]
$$\vec{\mathbf{a}}(t) = \frac{d^2x(t)}{dt^2}\hat{\mathbf{i}} + \frac{d^2y(t)}{dt^2}\hat{\mathbf{j}} + \frac{d^2z(t)}{dt^2}\hat{\mathbf{k}}$$
