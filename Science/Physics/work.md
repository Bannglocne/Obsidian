> **Work** is the [[energy]] used to cause an object to move against a [[force]]


> [!NOTE] Work done by a Force
> The work done by a force is the [[integral]] of the force with respect to [[motion#Displacement|displacement]] along the path of the displacement:
> $$W_{AB} = \int_{\text{path AB}} \vec{F}.d\vec{r}$$

 can be _positive_, _negative_, or _zero_, depending on whether the force is generally in/opposite the direction of the displacement, or perpendicular to the displacement.

---
**Work done by**
1. Constant Forces
$$
W_{AB} = |\vec{F}||\vec{r}_{B} - \vec{r}_{A}|.\cos \theta
$$
2. Kinetic Friction
$$
W_{fr} = -f_k |l_{AB}|
$$
3. [[Gravity]]
$$
W_{grav} = -mg(y_B -y_A)
$$
4. [[Spring Force]]:
$$
W_{spring} = -\frac{1}{2}k(x^2_B - x^2_A)
$$
