> We can combine these three [[state function|state functions]]—$E$, $P$, and $V$—to define a new state [[function]] called **enthalpy**. 
> It is particularly useful for discussing [[heat]] flow in processes that occur under constant (or nearly constant) pressure. 

$$
H = U + pV
$$
- **$H$**: Enthalpy
- **$U$**: [[Internal Energy]]
- **$p$**: Pressure
- **$V$**: Volume
