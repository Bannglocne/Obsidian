**Rydberg equation** allows us to calculate the [[wavelength|wavelengths]] of all the spectral lines of hydrogen:
$$
\frac{1}{\lambda} = (R_H)(\frac{1}{n^2_1} - \frac{1}{n^2_2})
$$
- $\lambda$ is the [[wavelength]] of a spectral line
- $R_H$ is the _Rydberg constant_ ($1.096776 \times 10{-7} m^{-1}$)
- $n_1, n_2$ are positive integers, with $n_2 > n_1$
