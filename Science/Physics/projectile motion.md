#physics #kinematics 
Problem-Solving Strategy:
1. Resolve the [[motion]] into horizontal and vertical components along the $x$- and $y$-axes. The magnitudes of the components of displacement $\vec{s}$ along these axes are $x$ and $y$. The magnitudes of the components of [[velocity]] $\vec{v}$ are $v_x = v\cos \theta$ and $v_y = v \sin \theta$, where $v$ is the magnitude of the [[velocity]] and $\theta$ is its direction relative to the horizontal
2. Treat the [[motion]] as two independent [[motion#Motion in One Dimension|one-dimensional motions]]: one horizontal and the other vertical. Use the kinematic equations for horizontal and vertical [[motion]] presented earlier
3. Solve for the unknowns in the two separate [[motion|motions]]. Note that the only common variable between the [[motion|motions]] is time $t$. The problem-solving procedures here are the same as those for [[motion#Motion in One Dimension|one-dimensional motions]] kinematics.
4. Recombine quantities in the horizontal and vertical directions to find the total displacement $\vec{s}$ and [[velocity]] $\vec{v}$. Solve for the magnitude and direction of the displacement and [[velocity]] using:$$s = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}, \quad \theta = \tan^{-1}(y/x), \quad v = \sqrt{v_x^2 + v_y^2}$$ where $\theta$ is the direction of the displacement $\vec{s}$

- Horizontal Motion: $$v_{0x} = v_x; x = x_0 + v_xt$$
- Vertical Motion: $$y = y_0 + \frac{1}{2}(v_{0y} + v_y)t$$ $$v_y = v_{0y} - gt$$ $$y = y_0 + v_{0y}t + \frac{1}{2}gt^2$$ $$v^2_y = v^2_{0y} - 2g(y - y_0)$$
- Time of flight: $$T_{\text{tof}} = \frac{2(v_0\sin\theta_0)}{g}$$
- Trajectory: $$y = (\tan \theta_0)x - [\frac{g}{2(v_0\cos \theta_0)^2}]x^2$$
- Range: $$R = \frac{v^2_0 \sin2\theta_0}{g}$$
