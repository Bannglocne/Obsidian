## Dalton’s Atomic Theory
1. Each element is composed of extremely small particles called atoms.
2. All atoms of a given element are identical, but the atoms of one element are different from the atoms of all other elements.
3. Atoms of one element cannot be changed into atoms of a different element by chemical reactions; atoms are neither created nor destroyed in chemical reactions.
4. Atoms of one element cannot be changed into atoms of a different element by chemical reactions; atoms are neither created nor destroyed in chemical reactions

# Molecules
A molecule made up of two atoms is called a **diatomic molecule** ($O_2, H_2,...$).
Compounds composed of molecules contain more than one type of atom and are called **molecular compounds** ($CH_4,...$).

Chemical formulas that indicate the actual numbers of atoms in a molecule are called **molecular formulas**($H_2O_2, C_2H_4,...$). $\#$ ($HO, CH_2,...$) **Empirical formulas** give only the relative number of atoms of each type in a molecule

**Structural formula** shows which atoms are attached to which

# Ions
If electrons are removed from or added to an atom, a charged particle called an **ion** is formed. 
- **cation**: positive charged
- **anion**: negatively charged

_Note_: In general, metal atoms tend to lose electrons to form cations and nonmetal atoms tend to gain electrons to form anions. Thus, ionic compounds tend to be composed of metals bonded with nonmetals

**Ionic compounds** are generally combinations of metals and nonmetals
