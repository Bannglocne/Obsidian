#aqueous #chemistry #equation 
[[Solution|Solutions]] used routinely in the laboratory are often purchased or prepared in [[concentration|concentrated form]] (called stock solutions). [[Solution|Solutions]] of lower [[concentration|concentrations]] can then be obtained by adding water, a process called **dilution**.
$$
\text{Moles solute before dilution} = \text{moles solute after dilution}
$$
In laboratory situations, calculations of this sort are often made with an equation derived by remembering that the number of [[Avogadro's number and the mole|moles]] of solute is the same in both the concentrated and dilute solutions and that _moles = molarity * liters_
$$
\text{Moles solute in conc soln} = \text{moles solute in dilute soln}
$$

$$
M_{conc} \times V_{conc} = M_{dil} \times V_{dil}
$$
