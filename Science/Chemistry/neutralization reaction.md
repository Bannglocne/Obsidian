> When a [[solution]] of an [[acids|acid]] and a [[solution]] of a [[bases|base]] are mixed, a **neutralization reaction** occurs. The products of the reaction have none of the characteristic properties of either the acidic solution or the basic solution.

$$
HCl(aq)(acid) + NaOH(aq)(base) \rightarrow H_2O(l)(water) + NaCl(aq)(salt)
$$

=> In general, a **neutralization reaction** between an [[acids|acid]] and a metal hydroxide produces water and a [[salt]].
