Atom have three subatomic particles: _proton_, _neutron_, _electron_: ${\color{cyan}{}^{12 \text{ - Mass number}}} {\color{green}_{6 \text{ - Atomic number }}}{\color{red}\text{C - Symbol of element}}$
- **Electronic charge**: $+1.602 \times 10^{-19}$
- **Atomic mass unit ($amu$)**: $1.66054 \times 10^{-24} \text{ g}$
- **Angstrom (Å)**: 1 $\text{Å} = 1 \times 10^{-10}$
- **Isotopes** Atoms with identical atomic numbers but different mass numbers
- **Atomic weight** = $\Sigma$ [(isotope mass) * (fractional isotope abundance over all isotopes of the element)]
- In one **mole** of a substance, there are $6$.022 × $10^{23}$ (**Avogadro’s number**) atoms or [[molecules]].
