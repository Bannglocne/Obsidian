#accounting #economics 
> A **trial balance** is a list of [[Account|accounts]] and their balances at a given time.

- The trial balance proves the mathematical equality of [[Debit & Credit|debits and credits]] after [[Posting]].
- A trial balance may also uncover errors in [[Project/Accounting/Journal|journalizing]] and [[Posting]].
- In addition, a trial balance is useful in the preparation of financial statements.

![[Trial_Balance.png]]

# Limitations of a Trial Balance
The trial balance may balance even when:
1. A transaction is not journalized. 
2. A correct journal entry is not posted. 
3. A journal entry is posted twice.
4. Incorrect accounts are used in journalizing or posting. 
5. Offsetting errors are made in recording the amount of a transaction.


> [!NOTE] Note
> The trial balance does not prove that the company has recorded all [[Transactions]] or that the [[Ledger]] is correct.

# Locating Errors
Errors in a trial balance generally result from mathematical mistakes, incorrect [[Posting|postings]], or simply transcribing data incorrectly.

# Dollar Signs and Underlining
- Dollar signs are typically used only in the trial balance and the financial statements, not in [[Project/Accounting/Journal|journals]] or [[Ledger|ledgers]]. 
	- Generally, a dollar sign is shown only for the first item in the column and for the total of that column. 

- A single line (a totaling rule) is placed under the column of figures to be added or subtracted. 
	- Total amounts are double-underlined to indicate they are final sums.
