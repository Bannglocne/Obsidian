#economics 
> The fall in [[total surplus]] that is not offset by government revenue is called **deadweight loss**. This loss occurs because the [[tax]] prevents buyers and sellers from realizing some of the _gains from trade_ (mutually beneficial transactions that would have happened without the tax).
![[Deadweight_Loss.png]]

==Example==: Joe cleans Jane’s house each week for $100. The [[opportunity cost]] of Joe’s time is $80, and the value of a clean house to Jane is $120. 

$\Rightarrow$ Joe and Jane each receive a $20 benefit from their deal. 

$\Rightarrow$ The [[total surplus]] of $40 measures the gains from trade in this particular transaction. 

The government levies a $50 [[tax]] on the providers of cleaning services. There is now no price that Jane can pay Joe that will leave both of them better off after paying the tax.

$\Rightarrow$ Jane and Joe cancel their arrangement.

$\Rightarrow$ The government collects no revenue

$\Rightarrow$ The $40 is pure **deadweight loss**
