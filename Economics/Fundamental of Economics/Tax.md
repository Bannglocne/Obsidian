#economics #principles
> [[Government Policy|Governments]] use **taxes** to raise revenue for public projects

- ==Taxes discourage market activity==: When a good is taxed, the quantity of the good sold is smaller in the new [[equilibrium]]. 
- ==Buyers and sellers share the burden of taxes==: In the new [[equilibrium]], buyers pay more for the good, and sellers receive less.

![[How_the_Burden_of_a_Tax_Is_Divided.png|700]]

$\Rightarrow$ [[Tax Incidence|A tax burden falls]] more heavily on the side of the [[market]] that is less [[Elasticity|elastic]].

## Application of Tax
**[[Market#Elasticity|Elasticity]] Rule**: The more elastic (responsive) the [[supply]] or [[demand]] curves, the larger the [[deadweight loss]]. (When participants are highly responsive to price changes, they exit the [[market]] more easily in response to a tax, causing the [[market]] quantity to drop significantly and resulting in a larger loss of [[Total Surplus|surplus]].)

# Tariff
> A **tariff** is a tax on goods produced abroad and sold domestically.

A tariff raises the price of imported goods above the world price by the amount of the tariff. This reduces the quantity of imports and moves the domestic [[Market]] closer to its [[Market#Equilibrium|equilibrium]] without [[Trade]].

=> While domestic producers are better off and the government raises revenue, domestic consumers are worse off. The tariff creates **deadweight loss** because it distorts incentives and prevents mutually beneficial trades from occurring.
