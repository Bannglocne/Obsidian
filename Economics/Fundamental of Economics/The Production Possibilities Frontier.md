#economics 
> **Production possibilities frontier** is a graph that shows the combinations of output that the economy can possibly produce given the available factors of production and the available production technology.

![[The_Production_Possibilities_Frontier.png]]
(_[[Assumptions|Assume]] an economy that produces only two of goods - cars and computers_)
- The economy cannot produce the amount of cars and computers represented by point $C$.
- $A$, $B$, $E$, $F$: Representing _[[Efficiency|efficient]]_ outcomes
- $D$: Representing an _inefficient_ outcome

$\Rightarrow$ The Production possibilities frontier:
- Shows one [[Trade-offs]] that society faces and the [[trade-offs]] can change over time: [[A Shift in the Production Possibilities Frontier]]
- Shows the [[Opportunity Cost]] of one good as measured in terms of the other good.
- Simplifies a complex economy to highlight some basic but powerful ideas: [[scarcity]], [[efficiency]], [[trade-offs]], [[opportunity cost]], and economic growth.
