#economics #principles
> Governments use **taxes** to raise revenue for public projects. A key concept is <mark>tax incidence</mark>, which refers to how the burden of a tax is distributed among buyers and sellers.

=> Taxes discourage [[Market|market]] activity. When a good is taxed, the quantity sold in the new [[Market#Equilibrium|equilibrium]] is smaller than it was without the tax.

## Application of Tax
**[[Market#Elasticity|Elasticity]] Rule**: The more elastic (responsive) the supply or demand curve, the larger the deadweight loss. (When participants are highly responsive to price changes, they exit the [[Market]] more easily in response to a tax, causing the [[Market]] quantity to drop significantly and resulting in a larger loss of surplus.)

# Tariff
> A **tariff** is a tax on goods produced abroad and sold domestically.

A tariff raises the price of imported goods above the world price by the amount of the tariff. This reduces the quantity of imports and moves the domestic [[Market]] closer to its [[Market#Equilibrium|equilibrium]] without [[Trade]].

=> While domestic producers are better off and the government raises revenue, domestic consumers are worse off. The tariff creates **deadweight loss** because it distorts incentives and prevents mutually beneficial trades from occurring.
