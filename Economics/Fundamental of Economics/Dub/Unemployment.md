# Identifying Unemployment
- **Employed:** Those who worked as paid employees, in their own business, or as unpaid workers in a family business.
- **Unemployed:** Those who were not employed, were available for work, and had actively tried to find employment in the previous four weeks.
- **Not in the Labor Force:** Those who fit neither category, such as full-time students, retirees, or homemakers.

=> Key Statistics:
1. **Labor Force** = Number of employed + Number of unemployed
2. **Unemployment Rate**: $\frac{\text{Number of Unemployed}}{\text{Labor Force}} \times 100$.
3. **Labor-Force Participation Rate**: $\frac{\text{Labor Force}}{\text{Adult Population}} \times 100$.

# Why There is Always Unemployment
Two types of long-run unemployment:
- **Frictional Unemployment:** Results because it takes time for workers to search for the jobs that best suit their tastes and skills. It is often short-term.
- **Structural Unemployment:** Results when the number of jobs available in some labor markets is insufficient to provide a job for everyone who wants one. This happens when wages are set above the [[Market#Equilibrium|equilibrium]] level.

=> Four Explanations for the Natural Rate
1. **Job Search:** The process of matching workers with appropriate jobs. **Unemployment insurance**, while protecting incomes, can inadvertently increase frictional unemployment by reducing the [[incentive]] to find a new job quickly.
2. **Minimum-Wage Laws:** When a minimum-wage law forces the wage to remain above the [[Market#Equilibrium|equilibrium]] level, it raises the quantity of labor supplied and reduces the quantity demanded, creating a surplus of labor (unemployment).
3. **Unions and Collective Bargaining:** Unions act as a cartel, bargaining for higher wages and better benefits. When unions raise wages above the [[Market#Equilibrium|equilibrium]] level, they create a surplus of labor in those industries.
4. **[[Efficiency]] Wages:** Firms may voluntarily pay above-equilibrium wages to boost worker productivity. This can improve worker health, reduce turnover, and increase worker effort and quality, but it also results in unemployment by keeping the wage high.
